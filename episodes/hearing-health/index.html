In this episode

What Episode 3 covers

Hearing is easy to take for granted. In this episode of On The Mend, Rebecca Bourke from Hearing Australia explains why hearing checks matter, starting with the fact that hearing loss happens so gradually that it is often a partner or family member who notices first.

She covers the early signs worth paying attention to, why more younger people are being affected through earbuds and headphones, protecting your ears in noisy places like mowing and worksites, what a hearing check involves, and the support Hearing Australia provides.

Key takeaways

The short version

  • ✓Hearing loss usually happens gradually, so someone else often notices first.
  • ✓Early signs include turning the TV up and asking people to repeat themselves.
  • ✓Keep the volume down with earbuds and headphones, and give your ears regular breaks from loud sound.
  • ✓Protect your ears in noisy places, like mowing, power tools and worksites.
  • ✓Hearing loss is not just for older people. More younger people are being affected.
  • ✓A hearing check is quick and straightforward, and gives you a useful baseline.
The guest

Rebecca Bourke

Rebecca Bourke is a Community Service Advisor at Hearing Australia, supporting communities across Melbourne's south east, including the Mornington Peninsula. She joins Episode 3 to talk about hearing checks, the early signs of hearing loss, and looking after your hearing at every age.
